Activities I done through my Academic Journey

Amidst the unprecedented challenges brought forth by the COVID-19 pandemic, I had the privilege of contributing my skills as a firmware engineer to a remarkable non-profit organization dedicated to building low-cost ventilators. Joining forces with a passionate team, we embarked on a mission to design and develop a ventilator solution that could be accessible to communities in need, especially during the peak of the pandemic.

During my first year at university, I co-founded a non-profit organization called 'Anirban' with a group of friends, driven by our shared commitment to assisting the underprivileged and promoting blood donation. Our initiatives included organizing blood donation drives, providing essential supplies such as food and clothing to those affected by disasters like floods and cyclones, and raising funds to support impoverished communities during festive occasions like Eid and contributed to environmental sustainability by planting trees. Through 'Anirban,' we sought to make a tangible difference in the lives of those in need, embodying the spirit of compassion and community service.
